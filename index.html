About Me

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute (STRI) in Panamá. In my work, I investigate the eco-evolutionary interactions between fungi and trees, which are key processes for biodiversity regulation, reforestation, and sustainable forestry practices. My research is cross-latitudinal, including work in European forests and the American tropics, where the underexplored and exuberant biodiversity continues to fascinate me. My research integrates genomic data in plants and fungi together with traits to uncover the eco-evolutionary foundation of these interactions.

Currently, I use eDNA to infer spatial distribuitions of fungi on Barro Colorado Island (BCI) in the Republic of Panamá to understand their associations with tropical tree species.
